My Journey in Creating My Personal Portfolio Website

Over the past few weeks, I have been building my own personal portfolio website. As someone who wants to showcase my work and skills to the world, I realized that having just a digital resume wasn't enough. I wanted a space that truly represents who I am, so I challenged myself to build a website completely from scratch.

First, I started with the planning phase. I used Figma to design the user interface (UI) and layout. I spent days sketching wireframes, picking the right color palette, and deciding where to place my bio, projects, and contact form. Designing beforehand really helped me visualize the final look.

Once the design was ready, it was time to dive into the coding phase. I opened Visual Studio Code (VS Code) as my main text editor. I began writing the structure using HTML, styling it with CSS, and making it interactive with JavaScript. The journey wasn't always smooth sailing; I ran into plenty of bugs, especially when trying to make the website responsive on mobile phones. However, searching for solutions on Google and asking AI helped me fix those errors. After everything looked perfect on my local computer, I needed to make it live online. I used GitHub to manage my project files and track my progress.

Looking back, building my own portfolio website was a challenging yet incredibly rewarding experience. It not only boosted my technical skills but also taught me patience and problem-solving. Now, I am proud to share my personal link with friends and potential clients.
